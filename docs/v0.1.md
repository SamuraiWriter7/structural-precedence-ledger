# Structural Precedence Ledger v0.1

## Status

Initial public specification.

## Purpose

v0.1 defines a single machine-readable record for documenting:

1. the canonical name of a structural idea;
2. its origin identity and first-publication timestamp;
3. evidence supporting precedence;
4. the proposition and its falsifiable boundaries;
5. later structural convergence;
6. the difference between similarity and demonstrated influence;
7. attribution or future royalty routing.

## Normative principles

### 1. Precedence is not influence

A record MAY demonstrate that an idea was published earlier.

It MUST NOT claim direct influence without evidence of access,
citation, or documented use.

### 2. Convergence must include differences

Every convergence record MUST describe both similarities and
differences.

Similar vocabulary alone is insufficient.

### 3. Origin identity must remain stable

The following fields SHOULD remain stable after publication:

- `record_id`
- `originator_id`
- `canonical_uri`

Corrections SHOULD update the record without silently replacing
its history.

### 4. Evidence must be externally inspectable

Precedence evidence SHOULD use durable and independently
inspectable sources, including:

- repository commits;
- signed releases;
- archived snapshots;
- DOI records;
- books;
- timestamped publications;
- signed origin records.

### 5. Royalty routing is optional but explicit

v0.1 does not calculate payment.

It records whether attribution or value-return routing is enabled
and which policy applies.

## Relationship classifications

### `direct_influence`

Documented evidence connects the origin record to the later work.

### `probable_influence`

Access evidence exists, but direct reuse is not fully demonstrated.

### `independent_convergence`

Similarity exists without evidence of access or influence.

### `partial_convergence`

Only part of the structure overlaps.

### `conceptual_analogy`

Resemblance is mainly metaphorical or high-level.

### `unrelated`

Later audit found no meaningful structural relationship.

## Evidence states

Influence-related evidence uses the following states:

- `none`
- `unverified`
- `indirect`
- `documented`

These states must not be interpreted as interchangeable.

For example:

```yaml
evidence_of_access: "documented"
evidence_of_direct_influence: "unverified"

means that access is known, but direct influence has not been
demonstrated.

Non-goals

v0.1 does not:

establish legal ownership of an abstract idea;
automatically enforce attribution;
calculate contribution percentages;
prove causation from similarity alone;
replace patents;
replace copyright;
replace contracts;
replace academic citation systems.

Three claims that must remain separate

The ledger distinguishes three different claims.

1. Precedence

The structure was publicly recorded earlier.

This may be supported by:

repository commits;
releases;
publications;
archived pages;
books;
signed records.
2. Convergence

A later work contains a meaningfully similar structure.

Convergence analysis must record:

similarities;
differences;
scope boundaries;
confidence.
3. Influence

Evidence shows that the later work accessed, cited, reused, or
was otherwise influenced by the earlier structure.

Influence must not be inferred from similarity alone.

Design principles
Record origin without demanding fame.
Preserve attribution without blocking use.
Compare similarities and differences.
Never infer influence from resemblance alone.
Keep records inspectable by humans and machines.
Preserve correction history.
Prepare future connection to royalty systems.
Keep the origin identifiable even when the structure spreads.
