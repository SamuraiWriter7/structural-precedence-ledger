# Structural Precedence Ledger v0.4

## Prediction and Outcome Audit

## Status

Active specification.

## Purpose

v0.4 introduces a machine-readable method for recording
a prediction before its outcome becomes known and later
auditing that prediction against observable evidence.

The specification distinguishes:

```text
Prediction
    ↓
Observable indicators
    ↓
Success and failure criteria
    ↓
Outcomes
    ↓
Evaluation
Why this layer is necessary

A structural similarity identified only after an event may be
dismissed as hindsight or post-hoc interpretation.

A stronger record fixes, in advance:

the prediction;
its publication time;
its expected time window;
observable indicators;
success criteria;
failure criteria;
exclusions;
confidence;
precedence evidence.

Later outcomes can then be evaluated without silently rewriting
the original claim.

Prediction identity

Each audit has two identifiers.

PAU-YYYY-NNNN
PRED-YYYY-NNN

PAU identifies the complete audit document.

PRED identifies the original prediction contained within it.

Both identifiers must remain stable.

Prediction statuses
open

The expected outcome has not yet been evaluated.

An open prediction must use:

evaluation:
  result: "pending"
closed

The prediction has received a completed evaluation.

withdrawn

The prediction was withdrawn before ordinary evaluation.

A withdrawn prediction must preserve its original text and include
a withdrawal reason.

Outcome relevance

Every outcome is classified as:

supporting
contradicting
mixed
irrelevant

Outcome relevance and evidence strength are separate.

For example, weak supporting evidence must not be treated as
equivalent to strong supporting evidence.

Evidence strength
weak
moderate
strong

Evidence strength describes the reliability and directness of the
observed evidence.

Evaluation results
pending

No final judgment has been made.

confirmed

All success criteria were satisfied and no failure criteria matched.

partially_confirmed

Part of the prediction was supported, but not all success conditions
were satisfied or some contradictory evidence remained.

not_confirmed

The prediction failed against the declared criteria.

inconclusive

Available evidence is insufficient or contradictory.

withdrawn

The prediction was formally withdrawn.

Alignment score

The optional completed-evaluation score uses:

0.0 = no alignment
1.0 = complete alignment

The score supplements the categorical result.

It does not replace the written reasoning or matched criteria.

Confidence levels

Confidence combines a label and probability.

low     0.00–0.39
medium  0.40–0.69
high    0.70–1.00

The semantic validator rejects inconsistent combinations.

Temporal rules

The validator requires:

the expected window not to begin before prediction issuance;
the expected window end not to precede its beginning;
precedence evidence not to be created after prediction issuance;
outcomes not to predate the prediction;
evaluations not to predate recorded outcomes.
Criterion rules

Success criteria use:

SUC-NNN

Failure criteria use:

FLR-NNN

Evaluation records reference those stable criterion IDs.

Unknown or duplicate criterion references are rejected.

Forward audit lifecycle
Canonical Structure
        ↓
Prediction issued
        ↓
Timestamp evidence fixed
        ↓
Observation window opens
        ↓
Outcomes recorded
        ↓
Criteria evaluated
        ↓
Prediction audit closed
Non-goals

v0.4 does not:

guarantee that a prediction is scientifically valid;
convert correlation into causation;
prove that external actors used an earlier structure;
reward predictions automatically;
hide failed predictions;
allow retrospective rewriting of prediction text;
replace scientific peer review.
Core principle

A prediction gains credibility not by being remembered
after the event, but by being fixed before it.
