# Structural Precedence Ledger v0.5

## Royalty and Contribution Binding

## Status

Active specification.

## Purpose

v0.5 connects structural origin and precedence records
to contribution claims, usage receipts, allocation policies,
settlement records, and value-return routes.

## Core lifecycle

```text
Canonical Structure
        ↓

        ↓
Participants

Participants

A participant may act as:

originator
derivative-author
implementer
auditor
maintainer
distributor
funder
other

A participant role does not automatically determine payment.

Payment or attribution is based on accepted contribution claims
and the selected policy.

Contribution claims

Each contribution claim records:

participant identity;
contribution type;
description;
evidence;
assessment time;
weight;
acceptance status.

Supported contribution types include:

origin
derivation
implementation
validation
maintenance
distribution
funding
other
Claim status
proposed
accepted
rejected
disputed

Only accepted claims participate in weighted allocation.

Usage receipts

A usage receipt records an observable use of a structure.

Supported usage types include:

reference
derivation
implementation
commercial-use
noncommercial-use
training-use
other

A receipt may contain:

consumer identity;
usage quantity;
usage unit;
gross value;
currency;
related contribution claims;
evidence.
Allocation policies
attribution-only

No monetary distribution is performed.

The origin and contribution lineage remain visible.

voluntary-contribution

Value is returned voluntarily without an enforced calculation.

fixed-share

Accepted contribution weights must sum to 1.0.

weighted-contribution

Accepted contribution weights are normalized before allocation.

hybrid

Fixed minimums and weighted contribution logic may be combined.

custom

A separately documented allocation rule is used.

Reserve

A settlement may reserve part of the gross value for:

protocol maintenance;
audit infrastructure;
dispute handling;
community funds;
public-interest development.

The calculation is:

reserve_amount = gross_amount × reserve_rate
Settlement calculation
gross_amount
    =
reserve_amount
    +
distributable_amount

Participant allocation amounts must sum to the
distributable amount.

Participant allocation shares must sum to 1.0.

Origin minimum

origin_minimum_rate protects the structural origin
from being mathematically erased by downstream contributions.

This does not imply that the originator must receive all value.

It ensures that an accepted and registered origin remains
represented in the allocation.

Settlement statuses
pending
simulated
approved
executed
disputed
cancelled
pending

No allocation has yet been calculated.

simulated

An allocation has been calculated but no payment occurred.

approved

The allocation has been accepted for execution.

executed

The value-return route has been used.

disputed

The settlement or contribution assessment is under dispute.

cancelled

The settlement will not proceed.

accepted origin contribution;
contribution weight rules;
currency consistency;
reserve calculation;
gross-value calculation;
allocation-share totals;
allocation-amount totals;
minimum origin allocation;
executed route availability;
audit chronology.
Non-goals

v0.5 does not:

create a legally binding contract by itself;
transfer money by itself;
determine tax treatment;
determine securities status;
replace copyright or patent law;
infer contribution without evidence;
guarantee that a declared usage receipt is truthful;
eliminate the need for dispute resolution.
Core principle

Open the structure.
Preserve the origin.
Measure the contribution.
Record the usage.
Return value to the source.
